\section{Conclusion}

MICO reframes LLM-assisted RTL integration as compiler-checked module composition over interfaces, contracts, clock domains, adapters, and compose graphs. The model proposes; the compiler, schemas, RTL tools, and benchmark gates decide.

The current artifact demonstrates a deterministic path through parsing, checking, typed IR, traceable SV/SVA emission, 83 expanded deterministic public-development tasks, a 40-task expanded deterministic held-out split, a 30-task supplemental realism manifest, 46 public simulations, 40 public bounded formal smoke checks, Yosys QoR, a Vivado out-of-context subset covering 21 reference-enabled split rows through 12 unique task pairs, and sanitized authenticated LLM records, with numeric evidence mapped in \texttt{docs/release\_claim\_table.md}. Under the tested OpenCode Go profiles and expanded v4 public-development, held-out, and realism manifests, schema-guided JSON AST prompting plus compiler-feedback repair improves positive-task compiler/lint success and unsafe rejection over direct RTL, SystemVerilog-interface, and MICO-source prompting.

The DAC submission boundary remains evidence-driven. The supported claim is a
tested structured-output and compiler-gated repair result, not unrestricted LLM
correctness. Full formal proof, CDC correctness, full timing closure, broad
Vivado QoR, arbitrary LTL, and model-general repair remain outside the claim
boundary until matching artifacts exist.
