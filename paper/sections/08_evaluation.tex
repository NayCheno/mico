\section{Evaluation: ModuleComposeBench}

ModuleComposeBench evaluates integration of existing RTL/IP modules: wrapper generation, stream chaining, bus attachment, width adaptation, protocol bridging, CDC adapter insertion, and unsafe ambiguity rejection. Metrics include first-pass compose success, lint/elaboration, simulation, bounded formal smoke, unsafe rejection, JSON AST equivalence, QoR delta, and repair turns.

The current artifact answers five evidence-bounded questions: whether intended compositions are accepted, unsafe compositions are rejected, accepted wrappers pass open-source RTL validation, selected ready/valid, transfer, width, no-drop/no-duplicate, register/status, protocol, and telemetry obligations survive bounded formal checks, and generated wrappers remain comparable with hand-written references.

For the tested profiles, the schema-guided MICO JSON AST repair baseline reaches
35--36/36 positive compiler and lint passes on the public-development split and
9--10/10 on the held-out split. It also rejects 25--26/26 public unsafe tasks
and 10/10 held-out unsafe tasks across the tested profiles. Direct Verilog and
SystemVerilog-interface prompts never reach positive lint pass rates comparable
to the structured baselines: their best rows are 5/36 public positives and 2/10
held-out positives. The MICO-source baseline produces MICO-looking text but has
at most 1/36 positive public compiler pass and 0/10 held-out positive compiler
passes in the matrix, illustrating that the structured JSON AST representation,
not merely the DSL vocabulary, is doing most of the work.

Repair is beneficial but bounded. Compared with direct Verilog,
\texttt{mico\_json\_ast\_repair} has 103 wins, 1 loss, and 82 ties on the
public split, and 27 wins, 0 losses, and 33 ties on held-out. Compared with
plain \texttt{mico\_json\_ast}, repair adds 17 public wins and 6 held-out wins,
with 2 public losses and 1 held-out loss; the net paired effect is reported in
Table~\ref{tab:llm-paired-comparisons} as wins minus losses divided by
comparable attempts. Two-sided exact paired tests over
discordant attempts give $p\le 1.04\times10^{-29}$ against the public
unstructured and MICO-source baselines, $p=7.29\times10^{-4}$ against plain
public JSON AST, $p\le 1.49\times10^{-8}$ against held-out unstructured and
MICO-source baselines, and no significant held-out difference from plain JSON
AST ($p=0.125$). All recorded repair-turn wins in this run are attributed to the
\texttt{deterministic\_adapter\_instance\_collapse} fallback, so the paper claims
compiler-feedback repair for a common adapter-as-instance error pattern rather
than broad autonomous semantic repair.
