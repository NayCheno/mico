\section{Conclusion}

MICO reframes LLM-assisted RTL integration as compiler-checked module composition over interfaces, contracts, clock domains, adapters, and compose graphs. The model proposes; the compiler, schemas, RTL tools, and benchmark gates decide.

The current artifact demonstrates a deterministic path through parsing, checking, typed IR, traceable SV/SVA emission, 62 public-development tasks, a held-out split, 36 simulations, 31 bounded formal smoke checks, Yosys QoR, a four-task Vivado subset, and sanitized LLM records. Its authenticated low-cost LLM result is negative, so the evidence-backed contribution is a benchmark, compiler gate, repairable representation, and failure-taxonomy workflow.

The DAC submission boundary remains evidence-driven: either the next matrix supports a positive structured-output and repair claim, or the paper should remain a negative LLM study plus compiler-gated benchmark/tool artifact. Full formal proof, CDC correctness, full timing closure, broad Vivado QoR, and LLM pass-rate improvement remain outside the claim boundary until matching artifacts exist.
