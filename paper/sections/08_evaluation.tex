\section{Evaluation: ModuleComposeBench}

ModuleComposeBench evaluates integration of existing RTL/IP modules: wrapper generation, stream chaining, bus attachment, width adaptation, protocol bridging, CDC adapter insertion, and unsafe ambiguity rejection. Metrics include first-pass compose success, lint/elaboration, simulation, bounded formal smoke, unsafe rejection, JSON AST equivalence, QoR delta, and repair turns.

The current artifact answers five evidence-bounded questions: whether intended compositions are accepted, unsafe compositions are rejected, accepted wrappers pass open-source RTL validation, selected ready/valid, transfer, width, no-drop/no-duplicate, register/status, protocol, and telemetry obligations survive bounded formal checks, and generated wrappers remain comparable with hand-written references.

For the tested profiles, the schema-guided MICO JSON AST repair baseline reaches
45--46/46 positive compiler and lint passes on the public-development split,
19--20/20 on the held-out split, and 15/15 on the realism split. It also rejects
36--37/37 public unsafe tasks, 20/20 held-out unsafe tasks, and 15/15 realism
unsafe tasks across the tested profiles. Direct Verilog and
SystemVerilog-interface prompts never reach positive lint pass rates comparable
to the structured baselines: their best rows are 6/46 public positives, 5/20
held-out positives, and 4/15 realism positives, with the SV-interface baseline
at 0 positives on all three splits. The MICO-source baseline produces
MICO-looking text but has at most 1/46 positive public compiler pass and 0/20
held-out or 0/15 realism positive compiler passes in the matrix, illustrating
that the structured JSON AST representation, not merely the DSL vocabulary, is
doing most of the work.

Repair is beneficial but bounded. Compared with direct Verilog,
\texttt{mico\_json\_ast\_repair} has 131 wins, 1 loss, and 117 ties on the
public split; 55 wins, 0 losses, and 65 ties on held-out; and 42 wins, 0 losses,
and 48 ties on realism. Compared with plain \texttt{mico\_json\_ast}, repair
adds 21 public wins, 9 held-out wins, and 6 realism wins, with 3 public losses,
1 held-out loss, and 0 realism losses; the net paired effect is reported in
Table~\ref{tab:llm-paired-comparisons} as wins minus losses divided by
comparable attempts. Two-sided exact paired tests over discordant attempts give
$p\le 4.89\times10^{-38}$ against direct Verilog on the public split,
$p\le 5.56\times10^{-17}$ on held-out, and $p\le 4.55\times10^{-13}$ on
realism; against plain JSON AST, the corresponding exact tests are
$p=2.77\times10^{-4}$, $p=2.15\times10^{-2}$, and $p=3.13\times10^{-2}$. The
authenticated v4 records include 29 accepted deterministic fallback records and
0 accepted free-form repair-patch wins, so the paper claims compiler-feedback
repair for a common adapter-as-instance error pattern rather than broad
autonomous semantic repair.
